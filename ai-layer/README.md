# AI Layer

<mark style="color:$primary;">**OpenAgriNet (OAN)**</mark> is designed as an open, interoperable digital public infrastructure that enables farmers to discover information, services, and entitlements across agriculture ecosystems in a unified manner. While the underlying network protocols and registries enable interoperability and decentralisation, Artificial Intelligence (AI) acts as the primary interface layer that translates farmer intent into actionable outcomes across the network.

The AI module in OAN is not a standalone chatbot or conversational feature. Instead, it functions as an intelligent orchestration layer that sits between user-facing applications and the underlying data sources, APIs, and network services. Its core purpose is to reduce complexity for end users, particularly farmers, by enabling natural language and voice-based interactions while preserving the modular, open-network architecture of OAN.

#### <mark style="color:$primary;">Why AI Is Central to OAN</mark>

Agricultural information and services worldwide are deeply fragmented across institutions, programmes, and data systems. Farmers often have to navigate multiple, disconnected sources to access critical information, ranging from government support schemes and advisory services to real-time market and weather data.

A key compounding challenge is that information is frequently not available in farmers’ language of choice. Even where digital services exist, they are often delivered in limited languages or technical formats, creating a digital and informational divide that disproportionately affects small and marginal farmers. This gap is not merely technological, but structural. It is driven by fragmented service delivery, inconsistent standards, and limited interoperability across systems.

In India, these global challenges are particularly pronounced due to the scale and diversity of the agricultural ecosystem. Farmers are required to navigate:

* Multiple schemes across central and state governments
* Advisory content dispersed across agricultural universities, research institutions, and KVKs (Krishi Vigyaan Kendras)
* Real-time data sources such as weather forecasts, mandi prices, warehouse availability, and soil testing services
* Status tracking and grievance redressal systems that are often programme-specific and siloed

Much of this information is spread across different portals, applications, and offices, often with limited language support and inconsistent user experiences. As a result, farmers frequently rely on intermediaries or informal networks, reinforcing existing inequities in access to information and services.

OAN addresses this fragmentation at the network and protocol layer. The AI module complements this by addressing fragmentation at the user interaction layer.

By enabling farmers to ask questions in their own language—using text or voice, the AI module:

* Interprets user intent
* Identifies the correct service or data source
* Makes tool calls to retrieve the right information&#x20;
* Presents responses in a contextual, understandable manner

In this sense, AI becomes the primary discovery and access mechanism for OAN-powered services.
